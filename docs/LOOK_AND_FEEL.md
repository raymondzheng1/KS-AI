# KidSmart AI — Look & Feel Brief

A creative-direction brief for the *vibe* of the app: the mood, the aesthetic, the feeling
a kid gets the moment it loads. Not a spec — a north star. The **logo is the soul**; this
brief is about translating its *feeling* into the whole experience.

---

## The feeling, in one line

> **A warm, sunlit world where a friendly star helps you discover AI — cool enough for a
> 12-year-old, kind enough to make every kid feel smart.**

If a screen makes a kid feel *curious, capable, and cheered on*, it's right. If it feels
cold, busy, generic, or babyish, it's wrong.

---

## Who's holding the phone

A **12-year-old**, on a phone, maybe in class, maybe on the couch. Old enough to roll their
eyes at anything that feels like a toddler app — young enough to love a character, a
celebration, and a leaderboard they can win. So the tone is **playful but smart**: warm and
fun, never cutesy or condescending. Treat them like a capable explorer, not a baby.

---

## The brand soul (read it off the logo)

Look at the logo and feel it before you analyse it: a **soft, hand-drawn yellow star** with
rosy cheeks and a big easy smile, wearing a graduation cap, sprouting a little plant, holding
a paintbrush, ringed by a blue orbit, with a soccer ball bouncing nearby. It feels
**handmade, optimistic, growing, and a little magical** — a friend who's excited to learn
*with* you, not a teacher lecturing *at* you.

Everything in the app should feel like it came out of the same sketchbook as that star:
**soft, rounded, warm, slightly imperfect, full of life.** Right now the app borrows the
logo's colours but has a flatter, more generic feel. Pull the whole thing back toward the
logo: hand-made warmth, not corporate flat.

---

## The world we're building

Imagine the app as a **place**, not a UI. It's a **bright, sun-warmed world of discovery** —
think the cosy optimism of a great kids' picture book crossed with the wonder of a science
club. Soft golden light, pillowy shapes, a winding path of adventures, a friendly star
mascot bouncing alongside you pointing at what's next. Knowledge feels like something you
*grow* (the sprout) and *play with* (the ball, the orbit), not something you cram.

Mood words: **sunny · soft · curious · encouraging · hand-made · alive · proud.**
Not: ~~clinical · flashy · neon · busy · babyish · corporate · edgy~~.

---

## Look & feel, by the senses

**Light & colour — warm and optimistic.** Sunny yellows and creams as the base, a clear sky
blue for knowledge, fresh green for growth, a friendly coral for "go," a touch of lavender
for a little magic. Soft, harmonious, lightly pastel — like morning light, not a highlighter.
Backgrounds breathe (lots of soft off-white); colour is used to *guide and reward*, not to
shout. Each of the 10 hurdles can own a colour, so the journey reads like a rainbow.

**Type — a friendly voice.** Headings are **rounded, chunky, and warm**, like the
hand-lettered logo — they should feel like a friend talking, not a textbook heading. Body
text is clean and effortless to read. Big, confident, generous. Never tiny, never stiff.

**Shape & texture — soft and tactile.** Everything is **pillowy and rounded** — squircle
cards, blobby buttons, organic edges, gentle hand-drawn touches (a sketchy underline, a few
scattered dots, the orbit swoosh). Slightly imperfect on purpose, so it feels made-by-hand,
not stamped-by-machine. Shadows are soft and warm, like sunlight — things feel touchable and
a little bouncy, never hard or boxy.

**The mascot — your buddy "Sunny."** The star isn't a logo parked in a corner; it's a
**character that travels with you.** It waves hello, points at your next hurdle, thinks
alongside you in a quiz, throws its cap in the air when you win, and gives a warm "let's try
that again" — never a frown — when you miss. Sunny is the emotional thread that makes the
whole app feel like a friend, not a worksheet. Keep its little symbols meaningful: the
**sprout = you're growing, cap = you're mastering it, paintbrush = create, orbit = curiosity,
ball = play & compete.**

**Motion — gentle and full of life.** Things ease in softly, bounce a little, breathe. The
orbit swoosh carries you between slides. Wins land with a satisfying pop; big wins burst into
confetti and a graduating Sunny. Energetic at the celebratory moments, calm and unhurried
while reading. Never frantic, never blocking the task. (And it all settles to a calm, still
version for kids who prefer less motion.)

---

## How the key moments should *feel*

- **Arriving (home/onboarding):** *"Oh, this looks fun — and it's for me."* A warm welcome,
  Sunny waving, an obvious, inviting "let's go."
- **The journey (the hurdle map):** *"I can see how far I've come and what's next."* A
  delightful winding adventure trail, your next stop glowing, Sunny pointing the way, your
  progress visibly *growing*.
- **Learning (the slide lessons):** *"This is clear and I'm not overwhelmed."* Calm, roomy,
  one idea at a time, beautiful — reading should feel easy and a little magical, never dense.
- **Getting it right:** *"Yes!"* A small, joyful burst — sound-of-a-smile feedback.
- **Getting it wrong:** *"That's okay — try again."* Warm and encouraging, zero shame. A kid
  should feel *coached*, never *corrected*.
- **Clearing a hurdle:** *"I earned this."* The big payoff — celebration, XP, Sunny
  graduating, an exciting nudge to the next one.
- **The leaderboard / rooms:** *"It's on — friendly rivalry."* Bright, fun competition with
  friends; you can always find yourself; winning feels good, being behind feels motivating,
  never embarrassing.

---

## The taste test (do / don't)

- **Warm**, not flashy. · **Soft**, not boxy. · **Hand-made**, not corporate-flat.
- **Playful & smart**, not babyish or cutesy. · **Encouraging**, not preachy.
- **Calm while learning**, joyful while winning — not loud everywhere.
- **Confident, capable kid** energy — not "look how cute the children are."
- **Cohesive** — every screen unmistakably the same world as the logo. One sketchbook.

---

## If this app were…

…a **place**, it's a sunlit clubhouse for young inventors. …a **friend**, it's the
encouraging one who's genuinely excited you're learning. …a **feeling**, it's the little
thrill of *"wait, I actually get this."* …a **book**, it's the picture book you didn't want
to put down — but with a brain. Build that.
