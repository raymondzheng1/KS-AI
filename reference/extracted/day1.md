## Key Concepts
### 1. What Is Artificial Intelligence?
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of computer science that creates systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence — things like recognising images, understanding language, making decisions, and solving problems.
A simple way to think about it: a regular computer program does exactly what you tell it, step by step, every time. An AI system learns from examples and figures out its own rules.
Regular Program vs AI | 
Regular Computer Program | AI System
You write every rule | It learns rules from data
Rigid — breaks if input changes | Flexible — adapts to new inputs
Example: calculator | Example: voice assistant
Follows instructions | Recognises patterns

### 2. The AI Family Tree
AI is a broad term. Here are the key levels within it:
Artificial Intelligence (AI): The big umbrella — any machine that mimics human thinking
Machine Learning (ML): A type of AI where the system learns from data without being explicitly programmed for each task
Deep Learning: A powerful type of ML that uses networks inspired by the human brain (called neural networks) — this is what powers image recognition and ChatGPT
Generative AI: AI that can create new content — text, images, music, code — based on what it has learned
### 3. Narrow AI vs. General AI
This is one of the most important distinctions to understand:
Narrow AI | General AI
Narrow AI (what exists today) | General AI (science fiction, not real yet)
Excellent at ONE specific task | Can do any intellectual task a human can
Cannot transfer skills between tasks | Would learn, reason, and adapt like a person
Examples: Siri, Netflix recommendations, spam filters | Examples: HAL 9000, Terminator (movies only!)
Safe, controllable, widely deployed | Does not exist yet — still research only

### 4. How Does AI Actually Learn?
The most common type of AI today learns through a process called Supervised Learning:
You give the AI thousands of examples with correct answers (e.g., 50,000 photos labelled "cat" or "not cat")
The AI finds patterns in those examples
It builds a mathematical model of those patterns
When it sees a new photo, it uses that model to guess: cat or not cat?
Over time, with more data and corrections, it gets better and better
Think of it like studying for an exam: the more practice questions you do, the better you get. AI does the same thing — but it can do millions of practice questions in seconds.
💡  Key Insight for Students
AI is not magic. It is maths + data + computing power. / Every AI system was built by humans and reflects human choices about what data to use. / AI is a tool — like a hammer or a calculator. What matters is how you use it.

## Activity 2 — AI Myth Busters
📋  Instructions for Facilitator
Read each myth aloud. Students vote TRUE or FALSE by raising their hands or holding up a card. / Reveal the answer and give the brief explanation. Award points for correct answers. / This works best as a lively, fast-paced quiz — keep energy high!

MYTH 1: "AI is conscious and has feelings."
✅  BUSTED — FALSE
AI systems process information mathematically. They do not experience emotions, consciousness, or awareness. When ChatGPT says "I feel excited about this," it is generating text that sounds human — not actually feeling anything. This is called the Chinese Room problem (a famous thought experiment by philosopher John Searle).

MYTH 2: "AI knows everything."
✅  BUSTED — FALSE
AI only knows what it was trained on. If the training data stops at a certain date, the AI knows nothing after that. It can also be completely wrong (this is called a "hallucination") — it can confidently state false facts. Always verify AI answers from trusted sources.

MYTH 3: "AI will take all human jobs."
⚠️  PARTIALLY TRUE — IT'S COMPLICATED
AI will automate some tasks (repetitive, rule-based ones), but it also creates new jobs. The key insight: AI cannot replace human creativity, emotional intelligence, ethical judgment, or physical dexterity in complex situations. The future belongs to people who know how to work with AI — which is exactly what this course teaches you.

MYTH 4: "AI is always smarter than humans."
✅  BUSTED — FALSE
AI is superhuman at narrow tasks with clear patterns (chess, protein folding, image recognition). It fails badly at common sense reasoning, novel situations it hasn't seen before, and anything requiring genuine understanding. A 5-year-old can understand "the cup is on the table and the table is in the kitchen, so the cup is in the kitchen" — most AI still struggles with this kind of reasoning.

MYTH 5: "AI makes unbiased decisions."
✅  BUSTED — FALSE
AI learns from human-generated data, which contains human biases. Amazon's hiring AI was found to discriminate against women. Facial recognition AI is less accurate on dark-skinned faces. AI inherits our biases and can even amplify them. This is why human oversight is always essential.
