## The Demo Day Mindset
💡  Advice for Presenters
Start with the problem, not the technology. The audience needs to care before they can be impressed. / Show the tool working live — even if it's imperfect. A live demo with one bug is more compelling than a flawless recording. / Speak to what you learned, not just what you built. Judges value insight over polish. / If something goes wrong during the demo, say so calmly and explain what it should do. Handling failure gracefully is itself impressive. / Thank your team, your facilitator, and anyone who gave you feedback during user testing.

## Audience Questions — Facilitator Guide
📋  Suggested Questions for Audience Members and Guests
What made you choose this particular problem to solve? / How did you decide which parts of the solution to give to AI and which to keep for humans? / What would happen if someone tried to misuse your tool? / If you had 3 more weeks, what would you build next? / What did AI do that surprised you during this project? / What did you discover that you wish you'd known before you started building?

🏆  Audience Choice Awards (all teams eligible for all categories)
Most Innovative Solution — tackled a problem in an unexpected way / Best User Experience — easiest and most enjoyable to use / Greatest Real-World Impact — could help the most people if fully built / Best Use of AI — most thoughtful and effective use of AI capabilities / Most Ethical Design — best handling of fairness, privacy, and safety concerns / Best Presentation — clearest storytelling and most engaging delivery

## Letter to My Future Self
📋  Facilitator Instructions
Provide each student with a piece of quality paper and an envelope. / Allow 15 minutes of quiet, reflective writing. Play soft instrumental music. / Students seal and address the envelope to themselves. Facilitators mail them in 6 months. / Tell students: "This letter will arrive when you are in the middle of a new school year. It is a message from the version of you who just finished this program."

📝  Letter to My Future Self — Prompts
Date: ___ / Dear Future Me, / Right now, here is what I understand about AI that I didn't two weeks ago: / The project I built was called [name]. Here is what I'm most proud of about it: / The moment that surprised me most during this program was: / The ethical question about AI that I still think about is: / By the time you read this letter, I want to have done these things with AI: / The one thing I want you to remember about AI is: / Signed, [your name], age 12
