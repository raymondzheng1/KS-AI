## Overview
You now understand what AI is and how it learns. But knowing about AI is not enough — you need to know how to use it effectively. Today we focus on the most important skill for working with modern AI: prompt engineering.
A prompt is the instruction you give to an AI. The same AI system can give you brilliant, useful answers or confusing, wrong answers — entirely depending on how you ask. This session teaches you to ask like a professional.

## Key Concepts
### 1. Why Prompts Matter So Much
Think of an AI language model like a brilliant expert who knows about almost everything. But this expert is locked in a room and can only communicate through written notes. How you write your note determines everything about the quality of the response.
Research shows that rephrasing the same question can change AI output quality by 50–80%. Prompt engineering is not a trick — it is a core professional skill.
### 2. The R-C-T-F-C Prompt Framework
Use this five-part structure to build powerful prompts:
Element | What It Means
R — Role | Tell the AI who to be. "You are an expert marine biologist with 20 years of experience teaching teenagers..."
C — Context | Give background. "I am a 12-year-old in Australia doing a school project on coral reefs..."
T — Task | State exactly what you want. "Explain how rising ocean temperatures affect coral bleaching..."
F — Format | Specify the output structure. "...in 3 short paragraphs with a summary bullet list at the end."
C — Constraints | Add limits. "...Avoid scientific jargon. Do not include information I cannot verify online."

### 3. Prompt Levels — From Weak to Expert
Level 1 — Weak Prompt: "Tell me about space."
Result: A vague, generic response that could fill a textbook — completely unhelpful.
Level 2 — Better Prompt: "Explain black holes to a 12-year-old."
Result: More targeted, but still quite broad.
Level 3 — Expert Prompt: "You are a science teacher for Year 7 students in Australia. Explain what a black hole is using one everyday analogy, three key facts, and a 'mind-blowing' closing statement. Keep the total under 200 words."
Result: Precise, useful, perfectly formatted response.
### 4. Advanced Techniques
Chain-of-Thought: Add "Think step by step" to get the AI to show its reasoning. Great for maths and logic problems.
Few-Shot Prompting: Give examples of what you want before asking. "Here are two examples of good haiku: [example 1], [example 2]. Now write one about summer."
Role Reversal: Ask the AI to ask YOU questions to clarify what it needs before answering.
Negative Constraints: Tell the AI what NOT to do. "Do not use bullet points. Do not include dates."
Iteration: Treat your first prompt as a draft. Read the response, then refine: "That's good but too long. Shorten it to 3 sentences and add one real-world example."
💡  The Professional Mindset
Expert AI users never accept the first response. They iterate. They refine. They experiment. Think of prompting like a conversation, not a single question. Each follow-up narrows the AI closer to exactly what you need.

## Activity 1 — Prompt Makeover Workshop
📋  Facilitator Instructions
Distribute the Prompt Makeover worksheet (or project on screen). / Students work in pairs — one writes a better prompt, the other tests it in ChatGPT/Claude. / Then compare: does the better prompt produce a better answer? Why or why not? / Each pair shares their best prompt makeover with the class.

📝  Prompt Makeover Worksheet
Instructions: Rewrite each weak prompt using the R-C-T-F-C framework. Then test both versions and compare the results. / WEAK: "Write a poem." → YOUR EXPERT PROMPT: ___ / WEAK: "Help me with my homework." → YOUR EXPERT PROMPT: ___ / WEAK: "What is climate change?" → YOUR EXPERT PROMPT: ___ / WEAK: "Give me a recipe." → YOUR EXPERT PROMPT: ___ / WEAK: "Explain gravity." → YOUR EXPERT PROMPT: ___ / Bonus Challenge: Write a prompt so specific that the AI produces an answer that surprises you with its quality.

## Activity 2 — The Prompt Battle Championship
📋  Facilitator Instructions
Divide into 4 teams. Each round, all teams get the SAME task (displayed on projector). / Teams have 5 minutes to craft the best prompt they can, then submit it to the AI. / Read all responses aloud (without revealing which team wrote which prompt). / Class votes on the best response. Facilitator awards points. / Play 5–6 rounds with different challenge categories.

🏆  Prompt Battle Challenge Cards
Round 1 — EXPLAIN: "Get the AI to explain how the internet works to a 5-year-old, using only a pizza analogy." / Round 2 — CREATE: "Get the AI to write the most suspenseful opening paragraph for a novel — no more than 80 words." / Round 3 — SOLVE: "Get the AI to help you plan a birthday party for 15 people with a budget of $100 AUD." / Round 4 — PERSUADE: "Get the AI to write the most convincing argument for a 4-day school week." / Round 5 — IMAGINE: "Get the AI to describe what daily life will look like in Australia in 2050 — be specific and surprising." / Round 6 — DEBATE: "Get the AI to argue both sides of this question: Should AI be allowed to create art for sale?"

📚  Key Vocabulary — Day 3
Prompt: The instruction or question you give to an AI / Prompt Engineering: The skill of crafting effective prompts to get high-quality AI outputs / Context Window: The maximum amount of text an AI can "see" at once / Chain-of-Thought: Prompting technique that asks AI to show its reasoning step by step / Few-Shot Prompting: Providing examples in your prompt to guide the AI's response style / Iteration: Refining a prompt based on the AI's response to get a better result / Constraint: A rule you give the AI about what NOT to do or include / Temperature: An AI setting that controls how creative/random vs predictable its outputs are
