# Facilitator Reference — Program at a Glance
Day | Theme & Focus
Day 1 | AI Is Already Around You — demystify, myth-bust, map AI in daily life
Day 2 | How AI Learns — ML training loop, Teachable Machine experiment
Day 3 | The Art of Talking to AI — prompt engineering framework, Prompt Battle
Day 4 | AI Eyes, Ears & More — computer vision, deepfakes, Spot the AI
Day 5 | When AI Gets It Wrong — hallucination, bias, Ethics Court simulation
Day 6 | AI + Creativity — generative AI, songwriting, art, creativity debate
Day 7 | Design Thinking Meets AI — problem framing, project selection, pitching
Day 8 | Build Day 1 — prototype sprint, user testing round 1
Day 9 | Build Day 2 — fix, polish, demo prep, presentation rehearsal
Day 10 | Demo Day & Graduation — showcase, awards, letter to self, next steps

📋  Essential Equipment Checklist
Laptops or tablets with webcams (1 per student minimum, 1 per 2 preferred) / Reliable internet connection (test speed before program) / Projector or large screen for class demos / Printed worksheets for each day (listed in each day's activity section) / Whiteboard or large paper sheets + markers / Sticky notes (at least 20 per student for Day 7 problem sprint) / Animal image cards for Day 2 sorting exercise (printed) / Timer (physical or on screen) / Reflection journals (1 per student — can be an exercise book) / Certificate paper for Day 10 graduation / Quality envelopes for Day 10 letters to self / Scoring sheets for Prompt Battle (Day 3) and Demo Day awards (Day 10)
