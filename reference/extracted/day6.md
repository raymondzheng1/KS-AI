## Key Concepts
### 1. How Generative AI Creates
Generative AI is trained to create new content rather than just classify or predict. The key technique is called a Diffusion Model (for images) or a Large Language Model / Transformer (for text and music):
For images (Stable Diffusion, DALL-E, Midjourney): Start with pure noise → the AI gradually de-noises the image, guided by your text prompt, until it produces a coherent picture that matches your description
For text (GPT-4, Claude): Predict the most likely next token (word fragment) given all previous tokens + training — do this thousands of times to produce a paragraph
For music (Suno, Udio, MusicGen): Similar to image generation — the AI has learned the patterns of pitch, rhythm, timbre, and structure from millions of songs, and generates new audio that follows these patterns
### 2. The Creativity Debate
Perspective | Argument
"AI is not creative — it remixes." | AI has never had an original thought. It recombines patterns from human-created works. True creativity requires consciousness, lived experience, and emotional intent.
"AI creativity is real — the output is new." | If the result is genuinely novel and valuable, does the process matter? Humans also build on existing knowledge and culture. The output is what counts.
"Human + AI is the real breakthrough." | The most creative work happens when humans use AI to explore possibilities they couldn't reach alone, then apply judgment, emotion, and intention to shape the result.

### 3. Copyright and Ownership
AI-generated content raises complex legal questions that are actively being debated in courts worldwide:
In Australia, copyright currently requires a human author — AI-only works may not be protected
If you use AI as a tool and make substantial creative decisions, you may hold copyright over the final work
Training AI on copyrighted artworks without permission is being challenged in multiple lawsuits globally
Platform terms vary widely — always check whether you own what you create on any AI platform

## Activity 1 — AI Creative Workshop
📝  Station A: AI Songwriting with Suno
Visit: suno.com (free tier available) / Write a prompt for a song: specify genre, mood, theme, and style. Example: "Upbeat pop song about discovering you can code, energetic, teenage perspective, with a catchy chorus" / Generate the song. Listen carefully. What do you like? What feels off? / Now humanise it: write new lyrics for the chorus yourself. What emotion do YOUR lyrics add that the AI version lacked? / Optional: Generate 3 versions with different genres (pop, hip-hop, classical) of the same theme. How does genre change meaning? / Reflection: What did AI do well? What did you add that made it yours?

📝  Station B: AI Visual Art with Adobe Firefly
Visit: firefly.adobe.com (free, no age restriction) / Generate an image that represents "the future of education." Use a detailed prompt with style, lighting, and mood. / Generate 4 variations. Select the one that resonates most. Write a sentence explaining why. / Now draw your own version of the same concept — freehand, 5 minutes. Don't try to copy the AI version. / Place both side by side. What did you choose to include that the AI didn't? What did the AI imagine that surprised you? / Reflection: Which version feels more "yours"? Why?

📚  Key Vocabulary — Day 6
Generative AI: AI that creates new content (images, text, audio, video) / Diffusion Model: AI architecture that generates images by reversing a noise process / Large Language Model (LLM): AI trained on vast text corpora to generate and understand language / Token: The basic unit an LLM processes — roughly a word fragment / Prompt (creative): A detailed description of the creative output you want the AI to generate / Copyright: Legal right protecting original creative works from unauthorised copying / Generative Adversarial Network (GAN): AI architecture using competing generator and discriminator networks / Latent Space: The mathematical space inside an AI where concepts are stored as relationships between numbers
